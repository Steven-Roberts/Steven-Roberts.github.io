Since middle school, I have a love for programming and math. I first started programming on a TI-87 graphing calculator where I created games like pong and one that would render fractals. These days, my programming language of choice has changed, but not my desire to pursue a career in the area of compute science and math has not.

One area in which I am most specialized is web development. In my four summers of interning at IIHS, I was able to quickly learn a broad range of languages, tools, and frameworks for creating websites. I worked effectively in both team and individual settings, and gained a wealth of practical, real-world job experience.

Scientific computing is the second area in which I specialize, in particular, methods for numerically solving differential equations. These sort of problems are of huge importance in understanding complex phenomena in physics, chemistry, meteorology, and many other disciplines. As an undergraduate researcher I was able to pick up new, cutting-edge numerical methods and abstracts concepts in order to implement them.
