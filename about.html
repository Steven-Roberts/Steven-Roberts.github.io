Since middle school, I have had a love for programming and math. I first started programming on a TI-87 graphing calculator where I created games like pong and a program that would render fractals. These days, my programming language of choice has changed, but not my desire to pursue a career in the area of computer science and math has not.

One area in which I am most specialized is web development. In my four summers of interning at IIHS, I was able to quickly learn a broad range of languages, tools, and frameworks for creating websites. I worked effectively in both team and individual settings and gained a wealth of practical, real-world job experience.

Scientific computing is the second area in which I specialize, in particular, methods for numerically solving differential equations. These sorts of problems are of huge importance in understanding complex phenomena in physics, chemistry, meteorology, and many other disciplines. As an undergraduate I focused many of my class on this area and started doing research with the Computer Science Laboratory at Virginia Tech. I had the opportunity to apply this knowledge during my latest internship, which was with NASA.

Education

I have a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science and a Bachelor of Science degree in Mathematics, both from Virginia Tech. My undergraduate GPA was 3.99. I am currently working on earning my Ph.D. in Computer Science also at Virginia Tech.

Hobbies

Outside of work and school, I enjoy mountain biking and solving puzzles. I also stay active by going to the gym, and I have recently picked up road biking.
